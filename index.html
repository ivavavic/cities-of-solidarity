The initiative From response to systems

Refugee inclusion, built into municipal systems.

Cities of Solidarity connects humanitarian assistance with long-term development planning. Under this umbrella, each participating city develops a Local Action Plan: an evidence-based, locally owned portfolio of costed actions that strengthen public services for all residents — not parallel humanitarian structures.

The cities Local Action Plans

Four cities, four plans.

Each plan is published in English, Romanian and Russian. Every city has a different inclusion pathway — explore how they compare in the Four Cities data story.

LAP · 2026 Comrat

An ageing, settled caseload where care comes first: community-based social care, health access, and housing repair — anchored in the capital of Gagauzia.

LAP · in progress Bălți

A family-heavy rental city in the north: school enrolment, childcare expansion and housing stability designed to serve host families equally.

The metropolitan pressure point at scale: rental formalization, housing-cost bridging, employment formalization and one-stop service navigation.

The self-reliance frontrunner in the south: consolidating employment gains through housing affordability, childcare for working parents and vocational Romanian.
