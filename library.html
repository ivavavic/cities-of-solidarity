The LAP Library.

Each Local Action Plan is published in English, Romanian and Russian. Every plan combines a data-driven City Profile with a portfolio of decision-ready priority actions — an operational roadmap for the city, and an investment pipeline for its partners.

Published · 2026 Comrat

The Comrat Local Action Plan for Refugee Inclusion sets out 12 priority measures across core municipal systems — from a Refugee Focal Point and integrated community care to a Social Rental Agency, mixed-income PPP housing and a Digital & AI Inclusion Hub. Grounded in the April 2025 City Profile, it responds to an ageing, settled caseload where care, health access and housing quality come first.

In progress Bălți

The Bălți plan is being developed with the municipality following the April 2025 City Profile and the Local Action Planning workshop. Its focus reflects a family-heavy rental city: school enrolment and curriculum bridging, kindergarten and creche expansion, childcare-linked activation for single parents, and tenure stabilization — designed to serve host families equally.

In progress Chișinău

Hosting the largest refugee caseload in the country, the capital's plan builds on the October–November 2025 City Profile (N=532 households). Its agenda is formalization at scale: rental-contract formalization with landlord incentives, housing-cost bridging tied to registered leases, employment formalization and skills recognition, and one-stop service navigation with digital support.

Published · 2026 Cahul

Built on the June 2026 Protection Community Profiling round, the Cahul plan consolidates the strongest self-reliance results among the four cities: housing affordability support transitioning to social-housing criteria, childcare expansion for working parents, vocational Romanian, and health-insurance enrolment through employers.
