About The initiative

Cities of Solidarity, explained.

Cities of Solidarity is a UNHCR-led initiative supporting Moldovan municipalities to integrate refugee inclusion into local governance, service delivery and development planning — in ways that strengthen systems for both refugees and host communities.

01 What it stands on

Six pillars of local ownership.

The initiative integrates refugee response into local governance, connecting humanitarian assistance with long-term development planning.

Inclusive municipal leadership

Municipalities, civil society and refugees co-design and manage the local response together, formalized through municipal Memoranda of Understanding.

Evidence-based planning

Socio-economic and spatial data identifies service gaps, guides investments and tracks shared outcomes — analysed at neighbourhood level.

Systems alignment

Humanitarian support reinforces existing public services rather than substituting for them. No parallel structures.

Humanitarian-to-development transition

Bridged financing: humanitarian funding catalyses sustainable municipal financing, integrating refugees into local budgets and plans as residents, not aid recipients.

Stronger local services for all

Every investment is tested against one question: does this make the city's local systems stronger and more self-reliant — for all residents?

Refugee participation & local coordination

Refugee Councils strengthen civic participation, and Local Refugee Coordination Forums connect local priorities to national strategies.

03 The engine

What are Local Action Plans?

Local Action Plans are practical municipal implementation and investment tools. They translate refugee inclusion from a policy objective into a sequenced, realistic and locally owned programme of action — an operational roadmap for the city, and an investment pipeline for its partners.

No parallel structures. LAPs strengthen existing municipal services and infrastructure rather than creating parallel humanitarian systems.

Grounded in evidence. Priorities are shaped by municipal strategies and budget realities, socio-economic and spatial analysis, and consultation with departments, providers, civil society and refugees.

Costed and fundable. Evidence is turned into decision-ready municipal actions with objectives, indicative costs, delivery pathways and measurable outputs.

A common reference point. The plan supports municipal planning, coordination, donor engagement, financing and phased implementation.

Dual benefit by design. Actions improve service quality and resilience for local residents while enabling refugees to move from dependency toward self-reliance and participation.

A living document. Each LAP is designed to be updated over time as needs, capacities and resources evolve — and is aligned with the National Programme for the Phased Integration of Foreigners 2025–2027.

04 Inside a plan

Two parts, one logic.

Section 1 · The evidence base

City Profile

A data-driven snapshot of refugee inclusion in the municipality. It combines household survey findings, local service and infrastructure data, and municipal development priorities — showing where refugees live, what challenges they face, and where local systems can be strengthened.

  • All collected data is georeferenced, so needs, services and opportunities can be mapped at neighbourhood level
  • Area-based analysis identifies exactly where support is needed, for whom, and why
  • Shows how refugee inclusion aligns with existing municipal development priorities
→
Section 2 · The investment portfolio

Action Plan

Translates the evidence into a structured set of municipal actions — typically 12 priority measures across core municipal systems — requiring coordinated implementation and shared support from national institutions, partners, civil society, the private sector and the refugee community.

  • Each action in a standardized, decision-ready format
  • Sequenced mix of quick institutional wins and larger phased investments
  • Doubles as an investment pipeline for donors, development finance and technical partners

Each action is presented with:

Action snapshot · location · cost · stakeholders · timeline Context & description Implementation steps Cost & timeline Key stakeholders Targets & indicators Impact & scale-up potential

Cross-cutting considerations — disability inclusion, child protection, gender-sensitive service delivery and social cohesion — are integrated throughout every plan.
